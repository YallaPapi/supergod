# L02 Funnel Architecture

## 1) Three in-lane options

### Option A: Lead Magnet -> Short Form -> Instant Booking (recommended structure)

**Stages**
1. Traffic from Reddit/Instagram/outbound DM lands on a single-offer page.
2. User claims a lead magnet: "30-day content calendar + 10 viral post templates for your niche".
3. Form step 1 asks only `name + email + handle`.
4. Form step 2 (progressive) asks `business type`, `monthly content budget band`, `primary channel`.
5. High-intent leads (budget/fit rules) see embedded booking calendar immediately; low-intent leads go to nurture sequence.
6. Post-booking page asks for assets needed for call prep (brand docs, competitors, goals).

**Lead magnet**
- Template pack + short teardown video.
- Fast to produce from your existing i2v system assets.

**Form logic**
- Progressive profiling: minimal fields first; qualification second.
- Hide nonessential fields until email is entered.

**Booking flow**
- Embedded scheduler on thank-you page for qualified leads.
- 2 reminder messages + 2-hour pre-call confirmation check.

**Evidence**
- Unbounce benchmark: median landing-page conversion rate is **6.6%** (Q4 2024 dataset; updated **March 4, 2025**). https://unbounce.com/average-conversion-rates-landing-pages/
- HubSpot knowledge base: form shortening shows only fields that cannot be enriched, explicitly aimed at increasing completion (last updated **September 29, 2025**). https://knowledge.hubspot.com/forms/use-form-shortening
- Calendly advanced booking forms support structured invitee questions and autofill behavior (updated **August 15, 2025**). https://help.calendly.com/hc/en-us/articles/14078682754071-Advanced-booking-form-features

**Notes**
- Expectation that progressive form steps will increase qualified-booking rate vs one long form is **UNPROVEN** for your specific audience until A/B tested.

### Option B: Direct "Book a Strategy Call" Funnel (no lead magnet)

**Stages**
1. Traffic lands on proof-heavy page (before/after examples, process, offer, guarantee).
2. Single CTA: "Book your content growth call".
3. Booking form includes qualification questions directly in scheduler.
4. Instant confirmation + short pre-call questionnaire.

**Lead magnet**
- None. Offer is immediate consultation value.

**Form logic**
- One-step booking form with required qualifiers (`niche`, `posting frequency`, `budget range`).

**Booking flow**
- Immediate slot selection.
- Auto-reschedule and reminder automation.

**Evidence**
- Gartner survey: **61%** of B2B buyers prefer overall rep-free buying experience; strong support for self-serve digital-first journeys (published **June 25, 2025**, survey fielded Aug-Sep 2024). https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-06-25-gartner-sales-survey-finds-61-percent-of-b2b-buyers-prefer-a-rep-free-buying-experience
- Calendly + LinkedIn press release: 165,000+ LinkedIn users already displaying Calendly links (published **March 20, 2025**), supporting native booking behavior. https://calendly.com/newsroom/press-release/calendly-teams-up-with-linkedin-to-help-professionals-source-more-client
- Unbounce benchmark baseline for page conversion context (updated **March 4, 2025**). https://unbounce.com/average-conversion-rates-landing-pages/

**Notes**
- Claim that removing lead magnet improves close rate for this service is **UNPROVEN** without pipeline-stage data from your niche.

### Option C: Interactive Diagnostic Funnel -> Segmented Offer -> Booking

**Stages**
1. Traffic lands on "Content Growth Diagnostic" quiz.
2. User completes interactive assessment (channel maturity, posting consistency, offer clarity).
3. Results page returns score + personalized 14-day fix plan.
4. If score indicates "execution gap," CTA is "Book implementation sprint call".
5. If score indicates "early stage," CTA is "Get starter package" + nurture.

**Lead magnet**
- Personalized diagnostic scorecard + action plan PDF.

**Form logic**
- Branch logic by user answers; asks only relevant questions.
- Collect contact info only before result reveal.

**Booking flow**
- Booking offered after high-intent score bands; lower bands sent to lower-friction offer.

**Evidence**
- Typeform help center states average completion rate of **47%** for typeforms and explains one-question-at-a-time plus logic flow rationale (evidence date: page accessed **March 3, 2026**; no explicit publish date shown) https://help.typeform.com/hc/en-us/articles/360029615911-whats-the-average-completion-rate-of-a-typeform
- Content Marketing Institute B2B research: case studies/customer stories and video are top-performing formats, supporting diagnostic+proof result pages (B2B 2025 report page, published in 2024). https://contentmarketinginstitute.com/b2b-research/b2b-content-marketing-trends-research-2025
- Gartner 2025 buyer data supports digital self-serve for early-stage research tasks. https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-06-25-gartner-sales-survey-finds-61-percent-of-b2b-buyers-prefer-a-rep-free-buying-experience

**Notes**
- Typeform completion benchmark transferability to your audience is **UNPROVEN**.
- Building/scoring logic may slow launch speed to first 10 clients.

## 2) Lane winner with explicit score math (0-100)

**Scoring model (weighted)**
- Speed to first 10 paying clients: 40%
- Build effort (lower effort = higher score): 25%
- Lead quality / qualification control: 20%
- Fit with your existing assets (i2v + automation + IG/Reddit infra): 15%

**Option A score**
- Speed: 8.5/10 -> 8.5 x 40 = 34.0
- Build effort: 7.5/10 -> 7.5 x 25 = 18.75
- Lead quality: 8.0/10 -> 8.0 x 20 = 16.0
- Asset fit: 9.0/10 -> 9.0 x 15 = 13.5
- **Total = 34.0 + 18.75 + 16.0 + 13.5 = 82.25/100**

**Option B score**
- Speed: 9.0/10 -> 36.0
- Build effort: 9.0/10 -> 22.5
- Lead quality: 5.5/10 -> 11.0
- Asset fit: 7.0/10 -> 10.5
- **Total = 80.0/100**

**Option C score**
- Speed: 5.5/10 -> 22.0
- Build effort: 4.5/10 -> 11.25
- Lead quality: 9.0/10 -> 18.0
- Asset fit: 8.0/10 -> 12.0
- **Total = 63.25/100**

**Winner: Option A (82.25/100)**
- Best balance of speed and qualification for the first 10 clients.
- Keeps funnel complexity low while still filtering poor-fit leads before calls.

## 3) Key failure modes + mitigations

1. **Failure mode:** Too many form fields reduce conversion.
- Mitigation: Keep step 1 to 2-3 required fields; move qualification to step 2 or booking form.
- Evidence anchor: HubSpot form shortening approach (2025 update).

2. **Failure mode:** High no-show rate after booking.
- Mitigation: Add SMS/email reminders, confirmation checkpoint, and a pre-call micro-task (upload account link + current posting cadence).
- Claim that this alone will reduce no-shows by a specific % is **UNPROVEN** without your historical baseline.

3. **Failure mode:** Wrong leads book calls (low budget / no urgency).
- Mitigation: Gate calendar behind explicit fit rules (`budget band`, `need start date`, `service scope`); route non-fit leads to nurture.

4. **Failure mode:** Lead magnet attracts freebie seekers.
- Mitigation: Align lead magnet tightly to paid service delivery (templates + implementation walkthrough), not generic broad-topic ebooks.

5. **Failure mode:** Channel-message mismatch (Reddit vs Instagram traffic intent differs).
- Mitigation: Use channel-specific landing variants and track `source -> booked call -> close` weekly.

## 4) Concrete 14-day execution outline (Option A)

**Day 1**
- Define ICP and strict qualification rules.
- Finalize one offer: "Content creation + posting ops retainer".

**Day 2**
- Build lead magnet v1 from existing i2v materials (template pack + 8-12 min walkthrough).

**Day 3**
- Write landing page copy (problem -> promise -> proof -> CTA).
- Draft two variants: creator-led businesses vs local service businesses.

**Day 4**
- Implement form step logic: short step-1 + qualification step-2.
- Add hidden fields for `utm_source`, `utm_campaign`, `platform`.

**Day 5**
- Configure booking flow and question set.
- Set confirmation + reminder automations.

**Day 6**
- Connect CRM sheet/pipeline with stage tracking: `Lead -> Qualified -> Booked -> Showed -> Closed Won/Lost`.

**Day 7**
- QA full funnel on mobile + desktop.
- Dry-run with 5 test submissions.

**Day 8**
- Launch outbound to warm network + previous contacts (20-30 targeted messages).
- Start Instagram CTA traffic to funnel.

**Day 9**
- Launch Reddit value posts with soft CTA to lead magnet page.
- Start daily KPI logging.

**Day 10**
- Review first data cut.
- If LP conversion <5%, simplify hero + reduce friction copy.

**Day 11**
- Tighten qualification thresholds if no-show or poor-fit bookings are high.

**Day 12**
- Add one trust asset (mini case study with concrete output metrics).

**Day 13**
- A/B test CTA wording: "Get templates" vs "Get 30-day plan".

**Day 14**
- Decision checkpoint:
  - Keep or replace lead magnet.
  - Keep or tighten booking gate.
  - Set next 14-day target: booked calls, show rate, closed clients.

**Operating KPI targets for first 14 days (starting targets, not guarantees)**
- Landing page conversion target: 6-10% (benchmark-informed; exact target for your niche is **UNPROVEN**).
- Lead-to-booked-call: 20-35% (**UNPROVEN** until traffic quality is measured).
- Booked-to-showed: 65%+ (**UNPROVEN** baseline).
- Showed-to-closed (first 10 clients push): 20-30% (**UNPROVEN** baseline).
