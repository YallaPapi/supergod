# L03 - Content Engine (hooks, scripts, repurposing loops)

## 1) Three in-lane options

### Option A: Daily Reactive Hook Engine (high-velocity short-form)

Design:
- Build a daily hook backlog (30-50 hooks/week) from recurring buyer pains, objections, and "myth vs reality" prompts.
- Produce 2-3 short videos/day (15-60s) using one-shot script skeletons: `hook (0-2s) -> proof -> CTA`.
- Post same-day across IG Reels + YouTube Shorts with light platform edits.

Why it can work:
- Short-form is still the top ROI content format in current marketer datasets.
- Massive Shorts consumption supports fast reach testing for new hooks.

Evidence:
- HubSpot 2026 marketing stats page (evidence date: 2026): short-form video is listed as highest-ROI format; top three ROI formats are video-heavy.
  - https://www.hubspot.com/marketing-statistics
- YouTube Blog (evidence date: June 18, 2025): YouTube Shorts averaging over 200B daily views.
  - https://blog.youtube/news-and-events/neal-mohan-cannes-2025/
- Wyzowl 2026 report (evidence date: late 2025 sample / 2026 publish): 69% of video marketers create social media videos; 71% report 30s-2m as effective length.
  - https://wyzowl.com/video-marketing-statistics/

Weak-claim flags:
- Claim that "2-3 posts/day is required to win" is **UNPROVEN** (platforms rarely provide universal posting-frequency thresholds).

### Option B: Pillar-to-Platform Repurposing Engine (weekly pillar -> multi-asset loop)

Design:
- Create 2 weekly pillar assets (8-12 min recorded breakdowns or long posts).
- Convert each pillar into a fixed derivative pack:
  - 6 short videos (problem, mistake, framework, case, objection, CTA)
  - 2 carousels/checklists
  - 1 authority post (LinkedIn/X)
  - 1 sales asset (audit CTA script)
- Tailor each derivative to channel format instead of literal repost.

Why it can work:
- Repurposing is explicitly a current top trend, and channel-specific adaptation outperforms one-size reposting.
- Meta’s Edits tooling directly supports scripting/storyboard/templating and cross-platform export without watermark friction.

Evidence:
- HubSpot 2026 State of Marketing article (updated Nov 18, 2025): repurposing across channels appears among top trends (35.08%); article notes difference between reusing same asset vs tailoring for platform.
  - https://blog.hubspot.com/marketing/hubspot-blog-marketing-industry-trends-report
- Meta Newsroom (Oct 1, 2024): Instagram Best Practices includes guidance on how often to post, attention capture, and reel length via creator education hub.
  - https://about.fb.com/news/2024/10/best-practices-education-hub-creators-instagram/
- Meta Newsroom Edits launch (Apr 22, 2025; updated Dec 17, 2025): storyboard support, templating, export with no added watermarks, and skip-rate feedback.
  - https://about.fb.com/news/2025/04/introducing-edits-streamlined-video-creation-app/

Weak-claim flags:
- Claim that "exactly 2 pillars/week is optimal" is **UNPROVEN** (operational heuristic, not validated benchmark).

### Option C: How-To + Proof Content Engine (education + testimonial loops)

Design:
- Build scripts around "how we did X" + client-proof snippets + implementation walkthroughs.
- Weekly cadence: 4 how-to shorts, 2 proof/testimonial cuts, 1 FAQ rebuttal thread.
- Repurpose each proof clip into outbound DM opener + landing page social proof snippet.

Why it can work:
- How-to formats consistently retain attention better than many video types.
- Reposts/credit mechanics on Instagram can extend originator reach when others share your content.

Evidence:
- Wistia 2025 State of Video (March 26, 2025): how-to videos showed strongest engagement benchmarks in their dataset.
  - https://wistia.com/learn/marketing/video-marketing-statistics
- Meta Newsroom (Aug 6, 2025): reposts are credited to original poster and may be recommended to re-poster followers.
  - https://about.fb.com/news/2025/08/new-instagram-features-help-you-connect/
- YouTube Shorts revenue-share update (Mar 28, 2024): 70B daily views at that time, confirming durable short-form volume before later 2025 growth.
  - https://blog.youtube/inside-youtube/shorts-revenue-sharing-update/

Weak-claim flags:
- Claim that testimonial-heavy creative always beats educational creative is **UNPROVEN** (varies by audience trust stage and niche).

## 2) Lane winner with explicit score math (0-100)

Scoring model (weighted total = 100):
- Speed to first 10 paying clients: 40
- Execution fit with current assets (i2v + Geelark + Reddit/IG infra): 25
- Evidence strength/recency: 20
- Compounding repurposing leverage: 15

Scores:
- Option A = 34 + 20 + 16 + 11 = **81/100**
- Option B = 33 + 23 + 18 + 15 = **89/100**
- Option C = 30 + 21 + 17 + 12 = **80/100**

Winner: **Option B (Pillar-to-Platform Repurposing Engine), 89/100**

Why Option B wins:
- Best balance of speed and sustainability: enough volume to generate leads quickly, without daily creative burnout.
- Strongest fit to available infrastructure (automation + cross-posting + content systemization).
- Highest compounding effect: one pillar produces multiple hooks/scripts/assets for outbound + inbound.

## 3) Key failure modes and mitigations

- Failure mode: Reposted-looking assets get weak distribution.
  - Mitigation: Keep clean masters, export platform-native variants, avoid third-party watermark artifacts, add native overlays per channel.
- Failure mode: Hook fatigue (same opening pattern reduces hold rate).
  - Mitigation: Maintain 4 hook families (pain, contrarian, proof, step-by-step) and rotate weekly; retire bottom quartile hooks every 7 days.
- Failure mode: High posting volume, low buyer intent.
  - Mitigation: Require explicit offer CTA in at least 30% of pieces (`audit`, `DM keyword`, `book call`) and track lead source.
- Failure mode: Team bottleneck at scripting/editing handoff.
  - Mitigation: Use fixed script templates + shot lists + batch days (record once, slice many).
- Failure mode: Vanity metrics without revenue signal.
  - Mitigation: Use funnel KPIs: `qualified DMs`, `audit requests`, `calls booked`, `close rate` by content pillar.

## 4) Concrete 14-day execution outline

Day 1:
- Define ICP segments (max 2) and 20 core pain points.
- Draft offer statement + CTA ladder (`free audit` -> `pilot package` -> `monthly retainer`).

Day 2:
- Build hook bank of 80 hooks (40 per ICP) using 4 families.
- Approve 6 script templates (problem-solution, teardown, case, objection, FAQ, CTA-only).

Day 3:
- Produce Pillar #1 (8-12 min) from highest-intent pain cluster.
- Extract 12 clip candidates; script 6 final shorts + 2 carousels.

Day 4:
- Edit/export platform variants; queue via posting infra.
- Publish first 3 shorts + 1 carousel + 1 authority post.

Day 5:
- Publish next 3 shorts; launch outbound using clips as openers (DM/email/Reddit context posts).
- Log responses and objections into script backlog.

Day 6:
- Review hold rate, saves/shares, profile actions, qualified DMs.
- Kill bottom 30% hooks; rewrite with new angles.

Day 7:
- Produce Pillar #2 focused on the strongest day 1-6 signal.
- Slice into same derivative pack.

Day 8:
- Publish Pillar #2 derivatives batch A.
- Add 2 testimonial/proof assets from conversations/results to date.

Day 9:
- Publish derivatives batch B.
- Run CTA sprint: every post includes one clear conversion path.

Day 10:
- Repurpose top 4 performers into alternate hooks (same core body, new opening).
- Ship Reddit-native and IG-native variants.

Day 11:
- Create mini compilation post: "3 mistakes we fixed this week" from prior clips.
- Push outbound follow-ups to all engagers from days 4-10.

Day 12:
- Second analytics checkpoint; update scorecard by pillar and hook family.
- Double down only on top 2 pillars.

Day 13:
- Produce Pillar #3 (fast-turn) strictly from winning pillar/hook combinations.
- Pre-build next 7-day queue.

Day 14:
- Weekly close sprint: book calls from warm DMs/leads.
- Publish transparent case/progress recap post with hard numbers and next CTA.
