# L03 - Content Engine (Hooks, Scripts, Repurposing Loops)

## 1) Three in-lane options

### Option A: Hook-First Short-Form Engine (fastest demand capture)
Design a daily short-form system where each post starts with a 1-line hook tied to a buyer pain point, then a 20-45 second proof/explanation, then a direct CTA to DM for a paid content package.

System:
- Hook bank: 50 hooks mapped to 5 pains (`no time`, `no ideas`, `low reach`, `inconsistent posting`, `no leads`).
- Script format: `Hook -> Problem cost -> 1 tactical fix -> CTA`.
- Repurpose loop: 1 script -> IG Reel + TikTok + YouTube Short + Reddit text post adaptation.

Evidence sources (with date):
- HubSpot, *State of Marketing 2026* (updated **2025-11-18**): short-form video reported as top ROI format; repurposing across channels also a top strategy. https://blog.hubspot.com/marketing/hubspot-state-of-marketing
- TikTok for Business, *Creative Best Practices for TikTok Ads* (undated page; evidence studies cited from **2020-2022**, accessed **2026-03-03**): emphasizes early branding/hook and fast attention capture in first seconds. https://ads.tiktok.com/business/en-US/blog/creative-best-practices-for-tiktok-ads
- YouTube Help, *Understand your audience retention* (help doc, undated, accessed **2026-03-03**): early drop-off analysis is core to improving short video performance. https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/9314415

### Option B: Proof-First Authority Engine (conversion-first)
Build content around mini case studies, teardown videos, and before/after posting systems to reduce buyer skepticism and raise close rates.

System:
- Content buckets: `mini case study`, `account teardown`, `process walkthrough`, `offer FAQ`.
- Script format: `Context -> What changed -> Why it worked -> CTA to book pilot`.
- Repurpose loop: long proof post -> 3 clips -> carousel summary -> Reddit value post.

Evidence sources (with date):
- Wistia, *2025 State of Video Report* (**2025-03-26**): reports strong use of educational/product and testimonial video formats, supporting proof-led content strategy. https://wistia.com/learn/marketing/2025-state-of-video-report
- Instagram Help Center, *How Instagram recommendations work* (help doc, undated, accessed **2026-03-03**): recommendation systems prioritize content likely to be relevant/engaging, supporting value-dense proof content for distribution. https://help.instagram.com/172948888998711
- Instagram for Creators, *Recommendations Guidelines* (undated, accessed **2026-03-03**): emphasizes original content and recommendation eligibility constraints relevant for proof assets. https://creators.instagram.com/recommendations-guidelines/

### Option C: Pillar-to-Micro Repurposing Engine (efficiency-first)
Produce 2 pillar assets per week (5-10 min videos or long posts), then systematically atomize into daily hooks/scripts/snippets across channels.

System:
- Pillar templates: `myth-busting`, `behind-the-scenes workflow`, `client journey`, `framework`.
- Script extraction: 1 pillar -> 8-12 short hooks -> 3 carousels -> 2 Reddit threads.
- Repurpose loop: edit once, distribute everywhere with platform-native formatting.

Evidence sources (with date):
- YouTube Official Blog, *More ways to create and remix Shorts* (**2022-04-21**): introduced Cut and remix tooling that operationalizes long-to-short workflows. https://blog.youtube/news-and-events/more-ways-to-create-and-remix-shorts/
- YouTube Official Blog, *New creation tools to inspire your next Short* (**2023-11-02**): expanded editing templates and faster short creation workflows for repurposing. https://blog.youtube/news-and-events/new-creation-tools-to-inspire-your-next-short/
- HubSpot, *State of Marketing 2026* (updated **2025-11-18**): repurposing content across channels reported as a top strategy. https://blog.hubspot.com/marketing/hubspot-state-of-marketing

## 2) Lane winner with explicit score math (0-100)

Scoring model (weighted for "first 10 paying clients" speed):
- Speed to launch: 40 points
- Likelihood to generate qualified inbound leads: 35 points
- Operational fit with existing assets (i2v + Geelark + Reddit/IG infra): 25 points

Scores:
- Option A: Speed 36/40 + Leads 27/35 + Fit 23/25 = **86/100**
- Option B: Speed 27/40 + Leads 31/35 + Fit 20/25 = **78/100**
- Option C: Speed 30/40 + Leads 22/35 + Fit 24/25 = **76/100**

Winner: **Option A (Hook-First Short-Form Engine)** with **86/100**.
Reason: fastest path to immediate distribution volume plus direct CTA loops, while still compatible with proof clips and repurposing.

## 3) Key failure modes + mitigations

- Failure mode: High views, low DMs (weak offer-to-content match).
  - Mitigation: enforce scripted CTA in every post (`DM 'SYSTEM' for 7-day posting plan`), track DM rate/post.
- Failure mode: Reused content gets suppressed due to non-original/watermarked assets.
  - Mitigation: export clean masters; platform-native edits and captions; follow recommendation/originality guidelines.
- Failure mode: Posting volume collapses after week 1.
  - Mitigation: maintain a rolling 14-day hook backlog and batch record/edit 2x weekly.
- Failure mode: Content attracts audience but not buyers.
  - Mitigation: bias topics toward buyer pain and buying triggers (`price`, `time`, `consistency`, `lead flow`).
- Failure mode: Claimed conversion uplifts from specific hook formulas do not hold in your niche.
  - Mitigation: treat formula-specific uplift claims as **UNPROVEN** until validated on your own accounts with 2-week A/B tests.

## 4) Concrete 14-day execution outline

Day 1:
- Define ICP + offer: one package, one CTA, one price anchor.
- Build 50-hook sheet (10 per pain cluster).

Day 2:
- Write 20 short scripts using `Hook -> Cost -> Fix -> CTA`.
- Create 4 CTA variants for testing.

Day 3:
- Record 10 videos in one batch (vertical, 30-45s).
- Edit master assets without watermark.

Day 4:
- Publish 2 posts each on IG + TikTok + YouTube Shorts.
- Post 1 Reddit adaptation (text-first, value-first, no hard sell).

Day 5:
- Publish 2 more shorts/channel.
- DM follow-up SOP: respond within 2 hours with qualifying questions.

Day 6:
- Publish 2 more shorts/channel.
- Review retention and hook hold rates; cut bottom 20% hooks.

Day 7:
- Publish 2 proof-led posts (mini teardown/case style).
- Offer a limited pilot slot (3 clients max).

Day 8:
- Batch-write next 20 scripts from winning hooks.
- Repurpose best Day 1-7 assets into new angle variants.

Day 9:
- Publish 2 shorts/channel + 1 Reddit post.
- Start outreach to engagers/commenters with soft CTA.

Day 10:
- Publish 2 shorts/channel.
- Run CTA A/B review (DM keyword A vs B).

Day 11:
- Record second batch (10 videos) from proven hooks.
- Add 3 objection-handling scripts (`price`, `trust`, `timeline`).

Day 12:
- Publish 2 shorts/channel + 1 proof carousel.
- Push booking link to warmed leads.

Day 13:
- Publish 2 shorts/channel.
- Compile weekly proof thread: wins, process screenshots, outcomes.

Day 14:
- Performance review and decision gate:
  - Keep only top 30% hook families.
  - Drop lowest-performing channel format.
  - Commit next 14-day cycle with proven scripts only.

Execution KPI targets for first 10 clients (working targets, not guarantees):
- 84+ short posts published in 14 days across channels.
- 3-8% DM rate from profile visits.
- 15-30 qualified sales conversations.
- Close 10 clients via pilot + monthly package upsell.

The KPI ranges above are **UNPROVEN** until validated against your actual audience, offer, and outbound response rate.
