## The Business Model

Faceless "YouTube Automation" (YTA) channels. No face on camera. AI scripts + AI voiceover + hired editor + stock footage = cash-generating media business. Run 10+ channels at once, each doing $2-10K/month, 80-95% profit margins.

**His philosophy**: "Build 10 channels that all do $5K/month rather than one channel that does $25K/month."

## 1. Niche Selection (80% of the work)

### The 8-Point Checklist

1. **Under 5 smaller channels** getting more views than subscribers (high demand, low supply)
2. **No channels with 100K+ subscribers** in the niche
3. **No smaller channels failing** in the niche
4. **Niche not older than 6 months** (2 months max for AI-visual niches)
5. **You can create better/longer content** than competitors
6. **Interest not required** if team has it
7. **Niche is monetizable** (check with NextLev, RPM > $4 minimum)
8. **Bonus**: Can post long videos (1-2 hours = highest RPM)

### Niche Research Methods

**Method 1 -- Homepage Method (Primary)**
1. Create fresh YouTube account solely for research
2. Search topics, click ONLY on faceless videos
3. Subscribe, like, comment to train the algorithm
4. Homepage fills with faceless channels
5. Use NextLev extension to filter by subscriber count (<60K)
6. Spend 1-2 hours/day doing this for 2 weeks

**Method 2 -- Niche Transfer**
Take a working US concept and replicate for Canada, Australia, UK, etc. Or transfer a format across topics (airport tips -> cruise tips, NBA format -> soccer format).

**Method 3 -- X/Twitter Leaks**
Search "faceless niche" on X. Look for posts with LOW view counts (<33 views) -- those niches haven't been widely exposed yet.

**Method 4 -- NextLev Trending**
Use advanced filters: low subscribers, first video within 4 months, sort by average views per video.

## 2. Video Creation Pipeline

### Step 1: Topic Research
- Go to competitor channels, look at RECENT outlier videos (not all-time popular)
- Search the topic on YouTube first -- if 5+ people covered it, skip
- "80% proven + 20% your twist"

### Step 2: Script (ChatGPT)

**Ideation Prompt:**
```
Pretend you are a YouTube guru and faceless channel expert. Write me a prompt
to copy this video style of ideas. I want perfect ideation model. Write me
video ideas based off these outliers that performed well on their channel.
Target high clickbait and CTR. Write me the expert prompt to give ChatGPT.
[Paste screenshot of competitor's top videos]
```

**Script Prompt:**
```
Here's a faceless YouTube automation niche I want to copy. Pretend you're a
professional script writing genius or guru. These are the best performing
videos so we can replicate their success and virality. Using the script I'm
going to provide as a reference. Write me a prompt to give ChatGPT to write
me a script but for this topic: [TOPIC]. Make sure it's insanely optimized
for AVD and viewer retention and flows the exact same as the reference I'm
about to provide. Make me a prompt and also include the script in the prompt.
[Paste competitor transcript as reference]
```

**Important additions:**
- "Without headings, without music instructions"
- "Make it about X words, ask me to continue after every 1,000 words"
- Use Deep Research mode for better accuracy
- 1,500 words = ~10 minutes of video
- Always target 1.5x competitor video length

### Step 3: Voiceover (GenAI Pro or ElevenLabs)

**Primary: GenAI Pro** (his current choice)
- 50M credits for $130
- 11M credits for $31
- V1 tier: 50 cents/day unlimited
- Voice cloning: upload competitor audio, clone it
- Filter by: gender, age, accent, quality

**Alternative: ElevenLabs**
- Higher quality but much more expensive
- V3 sounds more human
- Can create totally custom voices by describing them
- Good for unique voice identity

**Process:**
1. Download competitor audio with 4K Video Downloader
2. Upload to GenAI Pro to clone voice
3. Paste full script, generate voiceover
4. Import into Premiere Pro
5. Use FireCut plugin to remove AI pauses (settings: 120, then 100)

### Step 5: Editing

**Software:** Premiere Pro (recommended) or CapCut (free)

**Key:** "Video editing is the only task that cannot be done with AI. Hire a real human editor."

**Editor costs:**
- Simple (images + voiceover): $10-20/video
- Basic (stock footage + voiceover): $15-30/video
- Medium (some animation): $30-50/video
- Complex (character-based): $70-150/video
- Premium (branded, high animation): $200-300/video

**Where to hire:**
- Upwork (his #1 recommendation)
- Fiverr (acceptable, especially Nigerian editors)
- X/Twitter (highest quality, most expensive)
- Discord groups (cheapest)

### Step 6: Thumbnail

**ChatGPT Prompt:**
```
Pretend you are a professional thumbnail designer. Write me a prompt to copy
exactly the style of thumbnails. Target clickbait and CTR for the topic
[TOPIC]. Thumbnail text: [TEXT].
[Paste screenshot of competitor thumbnails]
```

**Tools:** Photoshop, Canva, ChatGPT image gen, OpenArt

**Rules:**
- 80% same as competitor style, 20% original
- Must be understood in 0.2 seconds
- One clear subject, strong emotion, high contrast
- Consistent format across all channel videos
- Red arrows, "breaking news" banners work well
- Never copy a competitor thumbnail 1:1 (fans already saw it)
- Test: paste your thumbnail into competitor's channel page -- if it blends in, it's good

### Step 7: Title & Description

- Copy competitor title patterns, add power words
- Power words: "exposed, destroyed, game over, robbing you blind, total ripoff, waste of money, avoid, stop buying"
- "People respond way more to negativity than to positive things"
- Use ChatGPT: "Can you make this more clickbaity?"
- Tags: rapidtags.io (paste title, copy generated tags)
- Description: Video title as first line, 4-5 line keyword-rich paragraph, channel description (same on every video)

### Step 8: Upload

- Upload as unlisted, wait 3-24 hours for HD processing, then publish
- Set country to where you want views from (US)
- Not made for kids (so people can comment)
- Category: closest match
- Video language: English
- End screens: one only (don't confuse viewers)

## 3. Channel Setup & Safety

### Account Types (ranked)
1. **Personal accounts** -- most powerful (years of YouTube trust)
2. **Brand new accounts** -- free, currently working well
3. **Aged accounts** -- agedprofiles.com, $4-6, accounts from 2006-2009
4. **Pre-monetized** -- premonetizedchannels.com, ~$595 (not recommended for beginners)

### Warmup Process (1 week minimum)
1. Use account as normal user
2. Watch 1-2 hours of content per day
3. Subscribe, like, comment on videos
4. Don't act like a bot
5. Have 1-2 warmed up channels ready at all times

### Anti-Shadowban
- Shadow bans are "100% real"
- Check impressions: browse/suggested = good; only search/external = bad
- If <100 impressions after 15 videos = shadowbanned
- Fix: delete videos, post same content to new channel
- Move channel to different (older) email via YouTube permissions

### Channel Protection
- Different email for every YouTube channel
- Different AdSense accounts (use family members)
- Never share passwords -- use YouTube Studio permissions (channel manager role)
- Add channel trailer showing creation process (demonetization prevention)
- For risky niches: private videos at end of month before potential ban

### Inauthentic Content Prevention
- Use personal name in channel name ("History with Stefan" not "Mystery Time")
- Add real person as profile picture
- Create channel trailer with real person speaking
- Use unique/cloned AI voice (not default voices everyone uses)
- Don't use identical thumbnail template every time
- Occasionally upload raw talking-head videos
- Have social media profiles linked

## 4. Testing Framework

### The 5-Video Test
1. Choose niche using criteria
2. Create 5 cheap test videos ($15-25 each, total $100-200)
3. Post pattern: post, skip, post, skip, post, post, skip, post
4. Wait 3-10 days
5. If one video takes off: continue posting daily
6. If nothing: diagnose (niche? topics? packaging? channel trust?)

### Test on 2 Channels Simultaneously
- Post exact same videos on 2 different channels at once
- Keep whichever performs better
- A video got 3 views on one channel, 1M on another

### Diagnosis Framework
- No impressions = bad channel (shadowban) -> repost to new channel
- Impressions but low CTR = bad packaging (thumbnails/titles)
- Good CTR but low AVD = bad content/too short
- If new channel also fails = bad niche -> restart research

## 8. Algorithm Rules

1. **YouTube rewards watch time** -- longer videos that keep people on platform win
2. **1.5x competitor length** -- always make videos longer than competitors
3. **CTR + AVD = everything** -- great thumbnail gets clicks, great script keeps attention
4. **70% of views come from recommendations**, not search
5. **YouTube pushes small channels now** -- algorithm works more like TikTok/Instagram
6. **20% of videos bring 80% of views** -- it's a numbers game
7. **One viral video changes everything** -- doubles down on outlier topics
8. **Never share videos with friends/family** -- pollutes algorithm data for 30 days
9. **Subscribers don't matter much** -- algorithm pushes based on content quality, not sub count
10. **Longer videos = higher RPM** -- more ad slots, YouTube makes more money
